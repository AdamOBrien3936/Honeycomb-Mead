About Mead

Mead is a fermented honey beverage that has been enjoyed for thousands of years. It is made by fermenting honey with water and sometimes fruit, spices, or grains. Mead can be still, sparkling, dry, or sweet, and comes in a variety of flavors.

At our meadery, we pride ourselves on creating unique and delicious meads that reflect the local terroir and the seasons. We use only the finest ingredients and traditional techniques to create meads that are both flavorful and balanced.

Types of Mead

  • Traditional Mead: Made with honey, water, and yeast. Often aged for a year or more to develop complex flavors.
  • Melomel: Made with honey and fruit. Can be dry or sweet, and the fruit can be added during or after fermentation.
  • Cyser: Made with honey and apples. Can be still or sparkling, and can be dry or sweet.
  • Metheglin: Made with honey and spices. Can include ingredients like cinnamon, ginger, or nutmeg.
  • Braggot: Made with honey and malted grains, like barley or wheat. Can be similar to beer or ale, depending on the ratio of honey to grains.

Recipes

Here are a few recipes to try at home:

  • Traditional Mead: Combine 1 gallon of water, 3 pounds of honey, and 1 packet of champagne yeast in a sanitized fermenting vessel. Cover and let ferment for 2-3 weeks, or until the fermentation activity slows down. Transfer to a secondary fermenter and age for at least 6 months before bottling.
  • Melomel: Combine 1 gallon of water, 3 pounds of honey, and 3 pounds of chopped fruit (like strawberries, peaches, or blackberries) in a sanitized fermenting vessel.
